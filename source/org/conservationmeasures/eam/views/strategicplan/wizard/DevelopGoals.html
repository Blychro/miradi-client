2A. Develop Clear Goals and Objectives

Develop Goals

Goals are linked to targets. To create a new goal, double click on a target and then click on the <goals> tab in the properties dialogue box. Click on the <create goal> button at the side of the page. Fill in the following fields:

  • Short Label: The text that will appear in the goal symbol on the diagram. Often a letter (e.g., "A" or "D").

  • Goal: The title of the goal. Generally 3-5 words (e.g., Maintain Seabird Populations)

  • Full Text: The complete goal statement.



To edit or delete a goal, click on the desired row and then either edit the text in the box at the bottom or click on the <delete goal> button.
 


A goal is a formal statement detailing a desired impact of a project. In conservation projects, it is the desired future status of a target. A good goal meets the criteria of being:

  • Linked to Targets: Directly associated with one or more of your biodiversity targets

  • Impact Oriented: Represents the desired future status of the target over the long-term

  • Measurable: Definable in relation to some standard scale (numbers, percentage, fractions, or all/nothing states)

  • Time Limited: Achievable within a specific period of time, often 10 or more years

  • Specific:Clearly defined so that all people involved in the project have the same understanding of what the terms in the goal mean
