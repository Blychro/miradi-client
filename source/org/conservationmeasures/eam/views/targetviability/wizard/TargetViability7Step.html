1B. Define Clear and Common Purpose

Describe Status of Targets

The next step is to determine the current status for each indicator.

For a selected indicator, fill in the following fields:

  • CS Rating:  What rating category your indicator is currently at.
  • CS Date: The date of the most recent indicator measurement.
  • CS Label: Brief description of the current value of the indicator or other short descriptor.
  • CS Text: Full description of the current value of the indicator.
  • Confidence:  Your confidence rating in the source of the indicator measurement.
  • Trend: Your assessment of the direction that this indicator is heading.

Miradi will automatically shade in the cell of the indicator ratings table with the color corresponding to your current status assessment. It will also display an arrow showing the trend, and the current status label.

The rating for this indicator will also appear on the appropriate row in the upper panel. Miradi will calculate a status rating for each KEA, based on the ratings of its indicators. The KEA ratings will be rolled up for each KEA type, and the average of the KEA type status ratings will be used as the viability status rating for the target. This target viability status rating will appear at the top of the properties dialog, and will be displayed in the diagram as a small ellipse next to the target.



The current status rating describes the indicator rating category where your key ecological attribute is today.

The four-category framework for categorizing the viability status for each KEA and target provides little opportunity to describe and keep track of incremental changes. The data fields for both current and future status allow you to record information on incremental change in indicators.

There is a big difference between an indicator that currently has good status, but is rapidly decreasing, versus one that has good status, but is staying constant or increasing. It is thus often useful to display the trend in the indicator. It is also important to specify your confidence in the indicator. The confidence ratings are defined as follows:

  • Rough Guess - Values are rough estimates by project team members. They are often an initial placeholder value meant to be improved down the road through use of other methods.
  • Expert Knowledge - Values are more refined estimates provided by experienced, reliable observers who know the local project conditions, but are not basing their estimates on any specific data or observations. These experts may be project team members, professionals managers, researchers, or members of a traditional community that have reason to take note of the conditions of interest and the experience necessary to provide reliable information. This category also includes educated guesses made by extrapolating data from other similar sites.
  • Rapid Assessment - Values are derived from data collected about this specific project area using relatively simple methods, including visual and auditory assessment. Data collection methods use protocols, field records and quality control procedures of varying degrees of standardization. They typically provide either presence/absence, categorial, or other other qualitative measurements of indicator values, or rough quantitative measurements.
  • Intensive Assessment - Values are derived from data collected about this specific project area using relatively detailed, quantitative methods. Data collection methods use standard protocols, field records and quality control procedures. They typically provide quantitative measurements of indicator values as well as an estimate of error.

Note that both rapid and intensive assessments can include data collected by non-project staff, or even public data available on the internet -- the key is that the data have to be about the project's specific site, targets, threats, and other factors, rather than extrapolating from other systems.
