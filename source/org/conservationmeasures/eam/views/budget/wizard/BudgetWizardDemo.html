5A. Analyze Your Data

Actual Expenditures - Demo

This version of Miradi does not yet support this feature.

Future versions of Miradi will allow you to generate reports on actual expenditures across different time periods by accounting code, activity, funding source, and against budgets.

It will include "Quicken" or "QuickBooks" style accounting functions and interface.



Actual expenditures are backwards looking reports on the money you spent for the activities/methods and tasks you undertook to implement your strategic and monitoring plans. They typically can be organized in several ways:

  • By Accounting Code - Reports of money spent in each accounting code category. These are useful for reporting to your organization's accounting department and to donors.

  • By Activity - Reports of money spent by activity, strategy, objective, and/or goal. This is the basis for Activity Based Costing (ABC) and is essential to improve management effectiveness.

  • By Funding Source - Reports of money spent by each funding source. These are useful for managing your cash-flow and for reporting to donors.

  • Against Budgets - Reports of money spent against the projections in your budgets. These are useful for managing your cash-flow and for improving your forecasting ability over time.
