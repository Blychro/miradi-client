4A. Develop Detailed Work Plans

Develop Activities and Tasks

Each strategy will have on or more activities associated with it. On the <Work Plan> tab, select a strategy that you want to focus on. Think about the specific activities that you will have to undertake to carry out this strategy. Click on the <Create Activity> button to add a new activity and then type the name of the activity in the label box in the bottom panel. Repeat for other activities for this strategy as needed. You can use the <Move Item Up> and the <Move Item Down> buttons to change the order.

If necessary, you can also highlight an activity and click on the <Create Task> button to add tasks under this activity. (If you are really detail oriented, you can even create sub-tasks under tasks.)

An activity is an action that is carried out to accomplish one or more of a project's strategies. Activities should capture a discrete package of work that is assigned to one or more specific individuals to complete over a relatively short time frame. Each activity should be defined such that:

  • It has clearly identified beginning and end points

  • The time and cost needs can easily be estimated

  • Its progress and completion can be easily assessed

  • It is distinct from other activities.

    • In many cases, breaking down a strategy into its component activities is a relatively straight forward process and the full suite of necessary activities can be identified. In some cases, however, where the work is more complex or new to the group, only the first few actions to launch the work may be apparent. In addition, if you find that an activity is difficult to define as outlined above, you may have to break it down into smaller tasks.
