Diagram

This view enables you to develop a conceptual model of your project. A good conceptual model graphically depicts your project's targets, direct threats, indirect factors, and interventions--and more importantly, the relationships between them.

As in any modeling exercise, you should not try to show all factors and relationships, but only the most important ones.

The Diagram interview is not functional yet, so you might want to shrink this interview panel by dragging its lower border upward.



 


Navigation Hints for the Diagram View

To add a new target or other factor, click the appropriate button on the tool bar (hexagon, rectangle, or ellipse), or use the drop down menu, or the right mouse button. The new factor will appear in the center of the page. If a factor is selected, you can then use the mouse or arrow keys to move it wherever you like.

Double clicking on a factor will bring up a menu in which you can name the factor, change its type, link an indicator or objective to it, or type a comment.

To link factors, indicating a causal relationship, click on the first, then hold the shift key and then click on the second. Then click the toolbar relationship button (the line with an arrow) to create the link.

The zoom-in and zoom-out buttons can be used to decrease or increase the size of the view.

Note that the scope box automatically forms around whatever targets you have active on the page.
