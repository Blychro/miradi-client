Diagram

This view enables you to develop a conceptual model of your project. A good conceptual model graphically depicts your project’s targets, direct threats, indirect factors, and interventions – and more importantly, the relationships between them.

As in any modeling exercise, you should not try to show all factors and relationships, but only the most important ones.

Click next to begin developing a model of your project.



 


Navigation Hints for the Diagram View

To add a new target or other factor, click the appropriate button on the tool bar use the drop down menu, or use the left mouse button. The new factor will appear in the center of the page. If a factor is selected, to reposition a factor

Double clicking on a factor will bring up a dialogue box in which you can name the factor, change its type, link an indicator or objective to it, or type a comment. Factors with comments linked to them will have a blue triangle in the corner. To link factors click on the first, hold the shift key, and then click on the second. Then click the relationship button to create the link. Or if you select a factor and then add a new one, they will be automatically linked.

The zoom-in and zoom-out buttons can be used to decrease or increase the size of the view.
