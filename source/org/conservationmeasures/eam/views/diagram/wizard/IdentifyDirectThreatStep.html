1C. Understand Project Context:

Identify Direct Threats

To insert a direct threat, select a target and then click the button. The software will add a new direct threat linked to the target and open a dialogue box.

Type the name of the direct threat. You can also add additional information about the threat in the comment box. You should also pick the entry that best describes your threat from the standard classification of direct threats in the drop-down menu.

Using the mouse or arrow keys, move the threat to wherever you want it to be.

 

Direct threats are primarily human activities that immediately affect a biodiversity target (unsustainable fishing, hunting, oil drilling, construction of roads, pollution or introduction of exotic invasive species).

They can also, however, include natural phenomena altered by human activities (global warming caused by fossil fuel use) or natural phenomena whose impact is increased by other human activities (a tsunami that threatens the last remaining population of an Asian rhino).

Hint: It is important to distinguish between the direct threats that immediately affect biodiversity targets and the indirect threats (also called root causes) that lead to the direct threats. In this step, you should only consider direct threats.

Hint: You may want to browse the Classification of Direct Threats for ideas of direct threats.
