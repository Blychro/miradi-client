1C. Understand Project Context:

Identify Indirect Threats and Opportunities

To insert indirect threats and opportunities, select a threat and brainstorm what you think is driving that threat. Select the threat and click the button.

The software will add a new indirect factor and open a dialogue box in which you can type the name and a comment. Using the mouse or arrow keys, move the factor to wherever you want it to be. In most models, factors further away from the target are placed out to the left-hand side of the page.

 

Indirect threats and opportunities (collectively indirect factors) are the economic, cultural, political, legal, social, and/or institutional factors that drive direct threats.

Hint: We use the same shape to represent both indirect threats and opportunities because often, a given factor (for example tourism) can be both a threat and an opportunity. Many people find it helpful to focus on neutral "factors" rather than trying to classify factors as being positive or negative.
