1C. Understand Project Context:

Identify Indirect Threats and Opportunities

Although for some direct threats there may be only one contributing factor, for others, there may be many. The idea is to construct chains showing the sequence of indirect threats and opportunities affecting your threats.

You can add additional factors to the chain by using the <Insert a Contributing Factor> button. You can add additional links by selecting one factor, holding the <Shift> key, selecting the second factor, and then clicking on the <Add a Link> button (the arrow).

You can also highlight a chain by selecting a direct threat factor, right-clicking the mouse, and picking the <Select Chain> command.

 

Chains of factors are the basic unit for project planning. Consider, for example, the following:

Target: Montane forest

Direct threat: Unsustainable logging

Indirect threat: Government policies promoting unsustainable logging

Indirect threat: Limited awareness within government of sustainable logging techniques (This indirect threat is really an underlying cause of the government policies that promote unsustainable logging.)
