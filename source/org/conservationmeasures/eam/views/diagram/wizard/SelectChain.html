2B. Identify "key factors" & draft strategies

Identify Key Factors: Select Chain

Strategies are linked to chains of factors showing the sequence of contributing factors affecting your direct threats and ultimately your targets.

To select chain that you want to focus on, select a factor in the chain, and the right (alt) click the mouse, picking the <Select Chain> command. All factors and links in the chain should now be highlighted. (If you need to add or remove factors from the chain, hold the <ctrl> key and click on the factors you wish to add or remove.)

Next, click on the <Show Only Selected Chain> button (available on either the toolbar or by right-clicking the mouse). This should hide all factors except the ones that you have selected.

Hint: A grey background on the diagram view means that you are showing only a selected chain and some factors are hidden.



 


True strategic planning involves using your conceptual model to determine at what points you will intervene - and also where you will not. The first decision that you have to make in determining your intervention points is to prioritize where in your conceptual model you need to take action. In some cases, the most obvious intervention point is the target or direct threat itself. But in many other cases, you get more leverage if you intervene on some of the indirect threats and opportunities affecting the direct threat - on the chain of factors affecting each direct threat.
